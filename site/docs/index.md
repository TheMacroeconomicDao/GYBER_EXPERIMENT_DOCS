## Key Concepts

### Principle of Minimum Individual Participation (PMIP)

With a sufficient number of participants, even minimal individual contribution enables implementation of projects of arbitrary scale. This principle mathematically demonstrates that collective micro-participation can rival and exceed traditional venture capital.

### Socio-Economic Selection (SES)

An evolutionary mechanism where projects compete not for investor capital, but for society's interest. Projects that serve genuine social needs attract participants; those that don't — naturally fade.

### Cybersocialization of the Economy

A historical process of transition of the global socio-economic mechanism to distributed peer-to-peer forms controlled directly by society. This process is as objective and irreversible as industrialization.
